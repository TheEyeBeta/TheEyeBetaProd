### Are the collision tables the same thing?

**No.** All three colliding tables have fundamentally different schemas (Section 4):

- `public.signals` uses `ticker_id`, `strategy_name`, `signal`, `confidence`, `entry_price` — a
  quantitative scoring/signal system.
- `theeyebeta.signals` uses `strategy_id`, `instrument_id`, `side`, `strength`, `features` — the
  new platform's agent-decision signal model.

These are **independent data models representing the same real-world concept
(trade signals) but designed separately**. No data migration between them is
appropriate without a deliberate mapping exercise.

The same holds for `exchanges` (different PK types, different column names) and
`corporate_actions` (completely different field names and semantics).

### Who is writing to public?

Section 8 shows ~1.4M inserts to `price_ticks` and ~1.2M inserts to `signals` in
this PostgreSQL session. Section 7 showed no active connections at query time —
writes happen in batches/bursts from a service that connects and disconnects.
The writing service is **not** from `TheEyeBetaProd` (which has written nothing to
public). It is System B's own data-ingestion workers.

### The correct interpretation of Section 12 failures in verify_full.py

The Section 12 checks in `db/verify_full.py` fail because they look for
`public.alembic_version` and tables named `exchanges / corporate_actions / signals`
in `public`. These are **System B's own tables** — they are not leaked copies of
theeyebeta tables. The check logic conflates "same name" with "same table", which
is incorrect given two systems share this instance.
